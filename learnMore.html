Team Overview

The New York Yankees, based in the Bronx, New York City, are one of the most famous and successful franchises in all of sports. Established in 1901 as the Baltimore Orioles (not related to the modern Orioles), the team moved to New York in 1903 and was renamed the Highlanders before becoming the Yankees in 1913.

Key Historical Moments

  • 1920: Babe Ruth is acquired from the Red Sox, marking the start of the Yankees dynasty.
  • 1923: Yankee Stadium opens. The team wins its first World Series.
  • 1936-1939: Yankees win four consecutive World Series titles.
  • 1950s: The Yankees dominate with players like Mickey Mantle and Yogi Berra.
  • 1996–2000: Yankees win four World Series titles in five years.
  • 2009: Yankees win their 27th and most recent World Series.

World Series Championships

The Yankees have won 27 World Series titles, the most in Major League Baseball history. Their championships came in the following years:

1923, 1927, 1928, 1932, 1936, 1937, 1938, 1939, 1941, 1943, 1947, 1949, 1950, 1951, 1952, 1953, 1956, 1958, 1961, 1962, 1977, 1978, 1996, 1998, 1999, 2000, 2009

Legendary Players

Babe Ruth

One of the most iconic figures in sports history, Ruth changed the game with his power hitting.

Lou Gehrig

Known as the "Iron Horse," Gehrig played in 2,130 consecutive games and was a powerhouse at first base.

Derek Jeter

A modern Yankees captain and 5-time World Series champion, Jeter was known for clutch performances.

Fun Facts

  • The Yankees' mascot is not a person — they are one of the few MLB teams without an official mascot.
  • Yankees players are famously clean-shaven due to a strict grooming policy.
  • The team has retired 22 numbers — more than any other MLB team.
  • They have appeared in the World Series 40 times — the most in MLB history.
